🖼️ Pixel Mode – The Complete Guide

Strategy · Tips · Common Mistakes · Example Puzzles · Win Faster

The Pixel Mode puts your visual memory to the hardest test: a heavily pixelated image of a Brawler – and you must figure out who it shows. With each wrong guess the image becomes slightly clearer, but you only have 8 attempts. This guide shows you how to recognize Brawlers from just a few pixels.

How does Pixel Mode work?

You start with an extremely pixelated image of a random Brawler. The image becomes progressively sharper – but only when you guess wrong. Your goal: identify the Brawler before the image is fully revealed, using as few attempts as possible.

Unlike Classic Mode, there's no attribute table as feedback – the image is your only clue. That makes Pixel Mode especially challenging.

Strategy: Reading a pixel image

1. Analyze the color palette

Even at maximum pixelation, colors are visible. Every Brawler has a characteristic base color:

  • Yellow/Gold: Brock, Tick, Chester, Amber, Piper
  • Blue/Teal: Gale, Squeak, Buzz, 8-Bit, Emz
  • Green: Spike, Sprout, Leon (partly), Bonnie
  • Purple/Dark Violet: Mortis, Tara, Crow, Gene
  • Red/Orange: El Primo, Ash, Ruffs, Frank (partly)
  • Pink/Rose: Max, Colette, Sandy (partly)
  • Brown/Earth tones: Bull, Jacky, Clancy
  • White/Bright: Nani, Lou, Surge

2. Check silhouette and shape

As the image clears slightly, you can make out outlines. Pay close attention to:

  • Headgear: Top hat (Mr. P), cowboy hat (Emz), cap (Stu, Surge)
  • Weapons: Large guns, lassos, fists – these stand out early
  • Body type: Massive tanks (El Primo, Bull) vs. slim figures (Leon, Crow)
  • Hair/hairstyle: Leon's distinctive mane, Emz's signature fringe
  • Unusual features: Multiple arms? Animal ears? Wings?

3. Background context colors

Official Brawler portraits often have colored backgrounds matching their rarity: orange tones for Legendary, blue tones for Super Rare etc. This can quickly indicate a rarity category.

💡 Pro Tip: Browse all Brawler portraits in the Brawler Library before playing. Your visual memory trains fastest through active viewing – not just playing.

Tips for Pixel Mode

  • Take your time on the first image: Try to assess color and rough shape even at maximum pixelation
  • Make mental notes: "Yellow, fairly small, weapon on the right" – this already narrows things down
  • Use strategic "test guesses": If you're sure about color but not shape, guess a Brawler with that color – the image will get clearer
  • Know the new Brawlers: Sirius (2026, dark/black-purple) and Najia (2026, sandy/beige with snake details) are the newest additions
  • Distinguish similar Brawlers: Nita and Poco look alike – pay attention to details like hair and clothing

Common mistakes in Pixel Mode

  • ❌ Guessing too early: After the first image you rarely have enough info – wait for 2–3 guesses for more clarity
  • ❌ Overestimating color: Some Brawlers have very similar colors (e.g. multiple yellow-orange Brawlers)
  • ❌ Confusing background with character: Focus on the figure itself, not the background
  • ❌ Forgetting new Brawlers: The game is regularly updated – know the latest additions
  • ❌ No system: When unsure, guess Brawlers from a color group, not randomly

Example puzzle – Pixel Mode

Imagine the hidden Brawler is Amber (Legendary, fire design, orange/red palette, slim figure with bottle):

  • Image Level 1 (max pixels): You see orange-red colors, somewhat slim shape
  • First thought: Brock? Chester? Amber? → All have orange/yellow tones
  • Guess 1: Brock (wrong) → Image clears, you now see the bottle and dress
  • Guess 2: Amber → ✅ Correct! Solved in just 2 guesses thanks to color and shape analysis
🏆 Goal: Try to solve the Brawler within 3–4 guesses. The best players recognize roughly 30% of Brawlers from the very first pixelated image.

Training: How to improve fast

  • Visit the Brawler Library and consciously study each Brawler for 5 seconds
  • Create mental categories: "Which Brawlers are mainly blue? Which ones have hats?"
  • Play daily – after 2 weeks you'll recognize most Brawlers at 50% pixelation
  • Focus especially on Brawlers you failed to recognize recently
